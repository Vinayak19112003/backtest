DRAWDOWN CALCULATION METHODOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Date Updated: March 2026
Applicable to: All Institutional Reports & Statistical Modules

1. THE PROBLEMATIC LEGACY METHODOLOGY (Peak Equity Anchoring)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Historically, drawdown metrics were calculated relative to the *Peak Equity* 
(using continuous `cummax()` logic). While standard in some contexts, this 
led to a significant flaw in assessing True Capital Risk.

Example of the flaw:
- Initial Capital: $100
- Run-up (Peak Equity): $200
- Drop to Valley: $170
- Legacy Calculation: ($200 - $170) / $200 = 15% Drawdown
- True Capital Loss: $0 (The account is still $70 in profit)

Because position sizing and risk of ruin are predicated on the INITIAL 
DEPOSIT, dropping from $200 to $170 does not threaten the original $100 
trading capital. Calculating risk based on peak equity exaggerated volatility
in profitable regimes while understating initial deposit risk during bad sequences.

2. THE NEW CORRECTED METHODOLOGY (Absolute Initial Capital Anchoring)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
All mathematical engines, statistical tests, and institutional reports 
now calculate Drawdown strictly against the Fixed Initial Capital base ($100).

Mathematical Definition:
1. `Equity = Initial Capital + Cumulative PnL`
2. `Absolute Drawdown ($) = Max(0, Initial Capital - Equity)`
3. `Drawdown (%) = (Absolute Drawdown ($) / Initial Capital) * 100`

Example of the correct behavior:
Scenario A (Profit Pullback):
- Initial Capital: $100
- Peak Equity: $200
- Fall to Valley: $170
- Correct Drawdown Calculation: $170 is >= $100. Drawdown = 0%.

Scenario B (True Loss):
- Initial Capital: $100
- Fall to Valley: $70
- Correct Drawdown Calculation: $100 - $70 = $30. Drawdown = 30%.

3. IMPACT ON METRICS & REPORTS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The adoption of Absolute Initial Capital Anchoring affects several 
institutional metrics in the reporting framework:

A. Max Drawdown % & $
   These figures now accurately reflect the MAXIMUM threat to the initial 
   deposit. A Max DD of 20% means the account dropped to $80 at its worst 
   point, regardless of any peaks it hit beforehand.

B. Average Drawdown
   Calculated EXCLUSIVELY from periods where equity < Initial Capital.

C. Drawdown Duration (Time Underwater)
   This now measures the duration (in days) the account spends below the 
   Initial Capital line, not the time spent below a rolling peak.

D. Calmar Ratio
   Now calculated as `CAGR / Max Absolute Drawdown %`. 

E. Monte Carlo Simulations
   Risk of Ruin (50%) and simulated drawdowns now rigorously reflect the 
   odds of the initial deposit falling below $50, accurately simulating 
   stress trajectories.

CONCLUSION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This methodological refinement ensures that risk metrics are structurally 
aligned with capital preservation objectives, preventing profitable 
volatility from inflating risk figures, and ensuring drawdowns reflect 
genuine threats to principal capital.
